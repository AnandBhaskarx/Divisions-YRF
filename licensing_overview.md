## licensing_queries

Yash Raj Films (YRF) operates a dedicated Licensing & Intellectual Property division responsible for the protection, management, and commercial exploitation of its intellectual property under the Indian Copyright Act and applicable international copyright conventions.

Scope of Licensing
Licensing covers monetization across:

- Television and satellite broadcasting
- Radio and audio platforms
- OTT and digital streaming services
- Consumer products and merchandising
- Advertising and brand partnerships
- Public performance and live events

Usage Authorization
Any third-party use of YRF-owned content—including commercial entities, individuals, or government bodies—requires a valid, advance license issued by YRF on mutually agreed commercial terms.

Third-Party Representation
Where strategically viable, YRF may represent select third-party intellectual properties for licensing, provided they align with YRF’s commercial objectives and brand values.

Governance
All licensing activities are centrally managed to ensure:

- Rights clarity
- Consistent valuation
- Brand protection
- Legal compliance

## Franchise

Several YRF films have evolved into recognizable characters and multi-film franchises, particularly within action, romance, and thriller genres.

Character Rights
Character licensing may include:

- Names and likeness
- Costumes and signature looks
- Stylized artwork and logos
- Iconic dialogues and phrases

Franchise Exploitation
Franchise programs may extend to:

- Multi-film universes
- Spin-offs and sequels
- Episodic and digital extensions
- Live events and experiential marketing
- Branded attractions and exhibitions

Experiential Integration
Franchise exploitation can be combined with YRF Studios infrastructure for:

- Experiential shoots
- Fan events
- Immersive branded content

Brand Protection
All franchise usage is governed by strict creative and brand guidelines.

## Portfolio

Yash Raj Films controls one of the most extensive film IP portfolios in Indian cinema, spanning multiple decades of Hindi film production.

Core IP Assets
The IP portfolio includes:

- Feature films and short-form content
- Scripts, screenplays, and story rights
- Film titles and franchise names
- Characters and narrative arcs
- Sound recordings, background scores, and music compositions
- Song videos and audiovisual clips
- Dialogues, lyrics, and iconic lines
- Artwork, posters, still photography, and promotional creatives

Ancillary Rights
Ancillary exploitation rights include:

- Home entertainment (DVD, Blu-ray, digital)
- Music publishing
- Digital and streaming exploitation

YRF Studios Assets
YRF Studios contributes proprietary assets such as:

- Production sets
- Sound stages
- Custom-built production design elements

These assets may be licensed independently or bundled with content rights.

## Commercial Structure

Licenses specify:

- Scope of rights
- Territory
- Duration

Financial Terms
Financial models may include:

- Upfront fees
- Minimum guarantees
- Royalties linked to usage or performance

Legal Provisions
Standard clauses cover:

- Approvals and brand usage
- Moral rights
- IP protection
- Indemnities
- Reporting and audit rights

Enforcement
Any unauthorized use of YRF content may result in legal action under copyright law.

## Music and Dialogue Licensing

YRF licenses its soundtracks, background scores, and song videos across:

- Films and television
- OTT platforms
- Advertising
- Radio
- Digital media
- Live events

Dialogue & Lyric Licensing
Dialogue clips, signature lines, and lyrics are licensed for:

- Brand campaigns
- Promotional videos
- Social media content
- Experiential marketing

Public Performance
Public performance licenses are issued for:

- Hotels and restaurants
- Retail outlets
- Events and exhibitions

These are often administered via authorized agencies such as Novex.

Special Rights
Synchronization, remix, and adaptation rights are granted on a case-by-case basis with clearly defined:

- Territory
- Duration
- Media
- Usage scope

## Remake Rights

Remake rights allow third parties to reproduce YRF stories in other languages or territories under separate agreements.

Adaptation Formats
Adaptations may include:

- Regional films
- International remakes
- Web series and television formats
- Stage productions
- Literary adaptations

License Structure
Each agreement defines:

- Language and territory
- Number of versions
- Creative approvals
- Revenue participation

Rights Retention
YRF typically retains sequel, prequel, and spin-off rights.
