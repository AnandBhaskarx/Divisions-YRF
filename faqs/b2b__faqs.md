## YRF B2B Partnership FAQs


Q1: What types of partnerships does YRF engage in?

YRF offers several partnership models:

1. Brand Integrations

* Product placements and brand visibility within films

2. Co-Marketing Campaigns

* Joint promotional initiatives leveraging film assets

3. IP Licensing

* Licensing YRF characters, franchises, or film properties

4. Event Partnerships

* Sponsorships for premieres, screenings, and promotional events

5. Co-Productions

* Joint film production ventures

6. Distribution Partnerships

* Territorial rights and distribution agreements

7. Music Licensing

* Use of YRF music in commercials and content

Each partnership type has specific commercial terms and processes.

––––––––––––––––––

Q2: How do I know which type of partnership is right for my brand or company?

Consider these factors:

If your goal is to increase brand visibility among film audiences
→ Recommended partnership type: In-film brand integration

If your goal is to launch a new product alongside entertainment content
→ Recommended partnership type: Co-marketing campaign

If your goal is to create consumer products based on film characters
→ Recommended partnership type: IP licensing

If your goal is to associate your brand with a premium entertainment event
→ Recommended partnership type: Event partnership

If your goal is to expand into film production or content creation
→ Recommended partnership type: Co-production

If your goal is to use YRF music in advertising
→ Recommended partnership type: Music sync licensing

Recommendation:
Schedule a consultation with the Business Development team to discuss your objectives. YRF will help identify the most effective partnership model.

––––––––––––––––––

Q3: What is the typical investment range for a brand partnership?

Investment ranges vary by partnership type and film scale.

In-film brand integrations:

* Small visibility: ₹1–3 crore
* Medium prominence: ₹3–8 crore
* Major integration with storyline involvement: ₹8–15 crore or more

Co-marketing campaigns:

* Typically require ₹2–5 crore minimum marketing spend
* Joint investment models are available

IP licensing:

* Upfront guarantee: ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore
* Royalty: 8–15% of net sales
* Duration: typically 3–5 years

Event sponsorships:

* Premiere events: ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore
* Multi-city promotional tours: ₹1–3 crore

Note: These are indicative ranges. Actual investment depends on film budget, star cast, expected reach, exclusivity requirements, and scope of partnership.

––––––––––––––––––

Q4: How far in advance should I contact YRF for partnership opportunities?

Recommended timelines:

For in-film integrations:

* Ideal: 6–12 months before film release
* Minimum: 4–6 months during production
* Too late: less than 3 months (post-production)

For co-marketing campaigns:

* Ideal: 3–6 months before release
* Minimum: 2 months

For event partnerships:

* Ideal: 2–3 months before event
* Minimum: 4–6 weeks

For IP licensing:

* Can be initiated anytime; not tied to a specific film

For co-productions:

* Ideal: 12–18 months before production start
* Development discussions can begin earlier

Pro tip: Early engagement allows better creative alignment and more integration opportunities.

––––––––––––––––––

Q5: Does YRF share its upcoming film slate with potential partners?

Yes, with appropriate confidentiality.

* Publicly announced projects are shared openly
* Projects in development require an NDA
* Scripts and detailed creative elements are shared only after an MOU
* Strategic slate details are shared with serious prospects during discussions

Process:

1. Submit initial inquiry
2. Sign NDA if required
3. Schedule partnership meeting
4. Receive relevant slate information

Yes. YRF actively welcomes international partnerships.

Key points for international brands:

Brand Integration Specific Questions

Q7: How does YRF ensure brand integration feels authentic and not forced?

YRF follows a creative-first approach:

* Story alignment: brand must fit the narrative
* Character consistency: product use must align with character profile
* Creative review by director and studio team
* No compromise clause if storytelling is affected
* Preference for organic placement over overt promotion

Philosophy example:

* Luxury car in spy film: natural
* Budget car in spy film: misaligned
* Luxury car in small-town drama: misaligned
* Two-wheeler in small-town drama: natural

Result: Authentic storytelling enhances brand perception.

––––––––––––––––––

Q8: Can we have category exclusivity?

Yes, category exclusivity is standard.

What it means:

* Only your brand appears in that product category within the film

Exclusivity scope:

* Film-specific
* Campaign-specific (2–3 months)
* Franchise-level exclusivity (premium option)

Cost impact:

* Typically adds 15–30% premium

Exceptions:

* Pre-existing background placements may remain, but no new competing brands will be added.

Q9: What deliverables do we receive as a brand partner?

Typical deliverables include:

Creative assets:

* Behind-the-scenes footage
* Still images
* Co-branded posters
* Digital and social media assets

Usage rights:

* Use of film association in advertising (with approval)
* Retail and digital activations
* Talent usage if separately negotiated

Promotional support:

* Brand logo on select materials
* Press mentions
* Event invitations
* Social media mentions

Campaign integration:

* Co-branded contests
* Joint digital campaigns
* Retail tie-ins

Measurement and reporting:

* Screen visibility analysis
* Social engagement metrics
* Post-campaign performance report

Specific deliverables depend on partnership level.

––––––––––––––––––

Q10: Can we get access to the film’s cast for brand campaigns?

Talent access is negotiated separately.

Standard partnerships do not include talent access.

Enhanced partnerships with talent include:

* Separate negotiation
* Higher premium
* Subject to talent availability

Typically feasible:

* 1–2 day shoot
* Event appearances
* Limited social media posts

Challenging scenarios:

* Extended commitments
* International shoots
* Conflicting endorsements

Cost expectation:

* ₹5–15 crore depending on talent and scope

Q12: What product categories are open for licensing?

Pre-approved categories include:

Consumer products:

* Apparel, accessories, collectibles, toys

Publishing:

* Comics, graphic novels, art books

Gaming:

* Mobile, console, AR/VR

Experiential:

* Live events, theme attractions, exhibitions

Food and beverage (selective):

* Limited-edition collaborations

Home and lifestyle:

Restricted or cautious categories:

Q13: What are typical licensing terms and royalty structures?

Standard framework:

Financial terms:

* Upfront MG: ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore
* Royalty: 8–15% of net sales

Contract duration:

* 3–5 years typical
* Renewal subject to performance

Territory:

* India only
* India plus select international markets
* Worldwide (premium)

Exclusivity:

* Category exclusive preferred

Quality control:

* Mandatory approvals
* Brand guideline compliance
* Manufacturing inspections permitted

Reporting:

* Quarterly reports
* Annual audit rights

––––––––––––––––––

Q14: How long does it take to finalize a licensing agreement?

Typical timeline:

* Initial discussion: 2–4 weeks
* Proposal evaluation: 2–3 weeks
* Contract negotiation: 3–6 weeks
* Approval and execution: 1–2 weeks

Total: 8–15 weeks

Q16: What is the typical budget range for YRF co-productions?

Tentpole films:

* ₹200–300 crore plus
* Partner stake: ₹50–150 crore

Mid-budget films:

* ₹80–150 crore
* Partner stake: ₹20–75 crore

Fresh talent films:

* ₹30–60 crore
* Partner stake: ₹10–30 crore

Digital series:

* ₹40–80 crore per series

Q17: What rights do co-production partners receive?

Rights depend on structure.

Typically include:

* Revenue share
* Credit placement
* Limited creative input
* Territory-specific distribution rights
* Audit and reporting rights

Final creative control usually rests with YRF.
