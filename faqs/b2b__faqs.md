## YRF B2B Partnership FAQs

Partnership Models Offered by YRF

YRF engages in multiple partnership models:

- Brand integrations (product placement and visibility)
- Co-marketing campaigns
- IP licensing (characters, franchises, film properties)
- Event partnerships (premieres, screenings, promotions)
- Co-productions
- Distribution partnerships
- Music licensing

Each model has specific commercial structures and engagement processes.

## Choosing the Right Partnership Model

Partnership selection depends on business objectives:

- Brand visibility → In-film brand integration  
- Product launch → Co-marketing campaign  
- Consumer products → IP licensing  
- Premium brand association → Event partnership  
- Content creation → Co-production  
- Advertising usage → Music sync licensing  

Recommendation: Schedule a consultation with YRF Business Development to identify the optimal model.

## Investment Ranges and Commercial Expectations

Investment varies by partnership type and scale.

Brand integrations:
- Small visibility: ₹1–3 crore
- Medium prominence: ₹3–8 crore
- Major storyline integration: ₹8–15 crore+

Co-marketing:
- ₹2–5 crore minimum spend

IP licensing:
- Upfront MG: ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore
- Royalty: 8–15%
- Duration: 3–5 years

Event sponsorships:
- Premieres: ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore
- Multi-city tours: ₹1–3 crore

Final costs depend on film scale, talent, exclusivity, and scope.

## Engagement Timelines and Planning Windows

Recommended timelines:

- In-film integration: 6–12 months pre-release
- Co-marketing: 3–6 months pre-release
- Event partnerships: 2–3 months pre-event
- IP licensing: Anytime
- Co-productions: 12–18 months before production

Early engagement enables deeper creative integration.

## Access to Film Slate and Confidential Information

- Publicly announced projects are shared openly
- Development projects require NDA
- Scripts shared post-MOU
- Strategic slate discussions held with qualified partners

Process:
1. Initial inquiry  
2. NDA (if required)  
3. Partnership meeting  
4. Relevant slate disclosure  

## International Brand Partnerships

YRF welcomes international brands.

Key strengths:
- Strong overseas theatrical presence
- High diaspora engagement
- Global Spy Universe performance
- Cross-border legal and commercial expertise

Common categories:
- Luxury automotive
- Fashion
- Technology
- Travel
- Premium consumer goods

## Brand Integration Philosophy and Authenticity

YRF follows a creative-first integration approach:

- Narrative alignment
- Character consistency
- Director and studio review
- No compromise on storytelling
- Preference for organic placement

Examples:
- Luxury car in spy film → natural
- Budget car in spy film → misaligned

## Category Exclusivity

Category exclusivity is standard.

Scope options:
- Film-specific
- Campaign-specific (2–3 months)
- Franchise-level (premium)

Cost impact:
- 15–30% premium

Pre-existing background placements may remain.

## Brand Partner Deliverables

Deliverables vary by partnership level.

Creative assets:
- BTS footage
- Stills
- Co-branded posters
- Digital assets

Usage rights:
- Film association (with approval)
- Retail and digital activations
- Talent usage (if negotiated)

Promotional support:
- Logo placement
- Press mentions
- Event access
- Social media exposure

Measurement:
- Screen visibility analysis
- Social engagement metrics
- Post-campaign reports

## Talent Access for Brand Campaigns

Talent access is negotiated separately.

Standard partnerships exclude talent access.

Enhanced access may include:
- 1–2 day shoots
- Event appearances
- Limited social posts

Cost range:
- ₹5–15 crore depending on talent and scope

## IP Licensing Portfolio

Available IPs include:

- Spy Universe (Tiger, Pathaan, Kabir)
- Dhoom franchise
- Classic films (DDLJ)
- Music catalog
- YRF brand assets

Approach:
- Selective
- Quality-first
- Open to international partners

## Licensed Product Categories

Pre-approved:
- Apparel, collectibles, toys
- Publishing
- Gaming (mobile, console, AR/VR)
- Experiential formats
- Limited F&B collaborations
- Home and lifestyle products

Restricted:
- Alcohol (case-by-case)
- Tobacco (not permitted)
- Gambling (not permitted)

## Licensing Terms and Royalties

Standard structure:
- MG: ₹50 lakh – ₹2 crore
- Royalty: 8–15%
- Term: 3–5 years
- Territory: India / International / Worldwide
- Exclusivity: Category-based
- Quality controls and audits mandatory

## Licensing Agreement Timelines

Typical duration:
- 8–15 weeks end-to-end

## Co-Production Framework

YRF co-produces under select conditions.

Models:
- Financial
- Creative
- Service-based

Evaluation criteria:
- Financial stability
- Track record
- Strategic and creative fit

## Co-Production Budgets and Partner Stakes

- Tentpoles: ₹200–300 crore (partner ₹50–150 crore)
- Mid-budget: ₹80–150 crore (partner ₹20–75 crore)
- New talent: ₹30–60 crore (partner ₹10–30 crore)
- Digital series: ₹40–80 crore

## Co-Production Rights

Partners may receive:
- Revenue share
- Credit placement
- Limited creative input
- Territory-specific rights
- Audit rights

Final creative control rests with YRF.

## Partnership Process and Response Timelines

End-to-end process:
- Initial response: 48–72 hours
- Assessment: 7–10 days
- Discussions: 2–3 weeks
- Proposal: 2–4 weeks
- Contract: 4–8 weeks

Total: 8–15 weeks

## Initial Inquiry Requirements

Include:
- Company profile
- Objectives
- Budget
- Timeline
- Geography
- Creative ideas
- Supporting materials

## NDAs and Confidentiality

NDAs are standard.

Options:
- YRF NDA
- Partner NDA (reviewed)

Typical execution: 3–5 business days

## Contact and Next Steps

Initial contact: YRF Business Development Team  
A dedicated partnership manager is assigned post-alignment.

## Evaluation Criteria and Outcomes

Criteria include:
- Brand alignment
- Commercial viability
- Audience relevance
- Creative integrity
- Operational feasibility
- Long-term potential

Possible outcomes:
- Proceed
- Request more information
- Decline (not aligned)
