## YRF Milestones – 1970

Foundation - **Yash Raj Films (YRF)** was founded in **1970** by **Yash Chopra** as an independent film-making company.

- The formation of YRF marked the beginning of a studio that would go on to **reshape Hindi cinema**, spanning storytelling, music, talent creation, global distribution, and studio-led filmmaking.

Significance - This year laid the foundation for what would become **India’s most influential and enduring film studio**, with a legacy spanning multiple generations of cinema.

## YRF Milestones – 1973

Cinema - **27 April 1973**: DAAG released.

- **First film produced under the Yash Raj Films banner**
- Directed by **Yash Chopra**
- Featured an unconventional and bold subject for its time — a man defying societal norms by living with **two wives**
- Starred **Rajesh Khanna**, **Sharmila Tagore**, and **Rakhee**
- Emerged as a **super hit**, praised for its performances and emotional depth

Legacy - _Daag_ established **YRF’s creative identity** early on — emotionally driven storytelling with the courage to challenge social conventions.

## YRF Milestones – 1976

Cinema - **27 February 1976**: KABHI KABHIE released.

- A grand romantic drama released at a time when such films were considered risky
- Showcased **Yash Chopra’s mastery of ensemble casting**
- Defied industry trends and **broke box-office records**

## YRF Milestones – 1981

Cinema - **14 August 1981**: SILSILA released.

- Created a historic casting coup with **Amitabh Bachchan, Jaya Bachchan, and Rekha**
- Explored the sensitive theme of **extra-marital relationships**
- Launched **Pandit Shivkumar Sharma** and **Pandit Hariprasad Chaurasia** as film composers
- Introduced **Javed Akhtar** as a lyricist

## YRF Milestones – 1984

Cinema - **9 February 1984**: MASHAAL released.

- A fiery social drama directed by **Yash Chopra**
- Gave him the opportunity to direct his favourite actor **Dilip Kumar**
- Featured one of **Anil Kapoor’s finest early performances**
- Praised for its realistic characters and emotional depth

## YRF Milestones – 1989

Cinema - **14 September 1989**: CHANDNI released.

- Revived the era of **romantic musicals** at a time dominated by action cinema
- **Sridevi’s white costumes** became iconic as the **“Chandni Look”**
- Songs became nationwide chartbusters

Cultural Impact - Extensive filming in **Switzerland** helped popularize it as a **tourist destination for Indian travellers**.

National Recognition - **Chandni** won the **National Award for Best Film – Popular & Wholesome Entertainment**.

## YRF Milestones – 1991

Cinema - **21 November 1991**: LAMHE released.

- Regarded by **Yash Chopra** as his **personal favourite film**
- A bold, unconventional love story spanning **two generations**
- Hugely popular in **metropolitan cities and overseas markets**
- Considered **ahead of its time** upon release

Recognition - **Neeta Lulla** won the **National Award for Best Costume Design**.

- In **2013**, on the occasion of **100 years of Indian cinema**, _Lamhe_ was ranked among the **Top Ten Romantic Films of the Century**.

## YRF Milestones – 1993

Cinema - **24 December 1993**: DARR released.

- A romantic psychological thriller that achieved **classic status**
- Featured **Shah Rukh Khan** in a bold, negative role that redefined the Hindi film anti-hero

National Recognition - **Darr** won the **National Award for Best Film – Popular & Wholesome Entertainment**, further cementing YRF’s reputation for mainstream excellence.

## YRF Milestones – 1995

Cinema - **20 October 1995**: DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE released.

- Marked the **directorial debut of Aditya Chopra** at the age of **23**
- Won the **National Award for Best Film – Popular & Wholesome Entertainment**
- Set records for the **highest number of Filmfare Awards** at the time

Cultural Impact - DDLJ began its **historic uninterrupted theatrical run**, becoming the **longest-running film in Indian cinema**.

- Widely regarded as:
  - One of the **most successful Hindi films of all time**
  - One of the **most loved films** by audiences and critics alike

## YRF Milestones – 1997

Distribution - Yash Raj Films launched its **own film distribution division**, strengthening control across the film value chain.

Cinema - **30 October 1997**: DIL TO PAGAL HAI released.

- Became a landmark romantic musical
- Reinforced YRF’s position as a leader in contemporary Hindi cinema
- It was a film Yash Chopra directed after a gap of six years. A romantic musical, the very successful film had modern dance as its base and became a favourite with the youth. Yash Chopra relaunched Uttam Singh as a film composer and the stupendous success of the music made it the best-selling album of all time.
- The film won three National Film Awards including 'Best Popular Film Providing Wholesome Entertainment', while Shiamak Davar won for the 'Best Choreography' and Karisma Kapoor won for 'Best Supporting Actress'

## YRF Milestones – 2000

Studio Transformation - Following the unprecedented success of its films, Yash Raj Films transitioned from being a film production house into a **professionally run and structured entertainment company**.

- A **CEO joined the management team**, initiating the process of building a full-fledged studio ecosystem.
- Distribution offices in India and overseas were **expanded and integrated** into a unified operational setup, laying the foundation for YRF’s global studio model.

Cinema - Directed by **Aditya Chopra**

- Launched **six new actors**, including **Uday Chopra**
- MOHABBATEIN Marked the **first on-screen collaboration** between **Amitabh Bachchan** and **Shah Rukh Khan**
- Celebrated for its dramatic scale, music, and emotional storytelling

## YRF Milestones – 2002

SAATHIYA released on 20 December 2002. It's a romantic drama directed by Shaad Ali and written by Mani Ratnam. The film stars Vivek Oberoi opposite Rani Mukerji in lead roles with cameo appearances by Shah Rukh Khan and Tabu. The music for the film was composed by A. R. Rahman.

YRF was chosen for the Golden Peacock National Quality Award for outstanding achievement in pursuit of Total Quality Management in the Entertainment Sector (Film/TV) for the year 2002.

YRF was one of the first production houses to deploy new and catalogue film content across the mobile ecosystem within India.

The Consulate General of Switzerland in Mumbai announced the first Swiss Filmfare Award, which was presented to Mr. Yash Chopra to commend his pioneering efforts in bringing Switzerland to Indian audiences by choosing the beautiful Swiss landscapes as a location for most of his films for almost 20 years thereby making Switzerland a popular tourist destination.

In 2002, The British Film Institute commissioned a book on this record-breaking film (DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE), under the BFI Modern Classics series. This is the first Hindi film to be chosen in this series of critical studies of international films produced over the last three decades, reflecting the enormous impact this film had on global Indian audiences.

## YRF Milestones – 2004

HUM TUM released on 28 May 2004. HUM TUM, a romantic comedy directed by Kunal Kohli and produced by Aditya Chopra, became immensely popular with the youth. The film stars Saif Ali Khan and Rani Mukerji in lead roles.

Saif Ali Khan won the National Award for Best Actor that year.

DHOOM released on 27 August 2004. DHOOM became a runaway hit, marking the first in the very popular franchise that was to follow in later years.

VEER-ZAARA released on 12 November 2004. The Yash Chopra directed love legend VEER-ZAARA was a cross border romantic saga that won the hearts of filmgoers all over the world. With a stellar cast, the film received accolades for its direction, screenplay, performances and music.

Yash Chopra was very successful in his experiment of creating a soundtrack based on unheard compositions of the late Madan Mohan, a composer who had passed away in 1975. This endeavour resulted in a best-selling album, various posthumous awards for the music and being hailed by the Billboard magazine as a historic achievement in the music industry. Like many of Yash Chopra's earlier films, VEER-ZAARA too won the National Award for Best Film providing popular and wholesome entertainment.

'YRF Music' label was launched overseas with the music of DHOOM and HUM TUM, and then with the release of VEER-ZAARA, a full-fledged in-house Music and Home Entertainment Division was launched, for India and global distribution. Apart from films produced by YRF, the new label also marketed film classics of Raj Kapoor, B.R. Chopra, Bimal Roy, Guru Dutt as well as blockbusters from contemporary cinema.

In addition to distributing their own productions, YRF also initiated domestic and international theatrical distribution of films from other reputed production houses.

Hollywood Reporter, the renowned international film magazine carried a survey on the Biggest Film Production Houses in the world and YRF appeared alongside big names like Buena Vista, Sony, Warner Brothers, Universal, New Line, Fox, Paramount, Miramax, MGM and DreamWorks just to name a few.

YRF featured on the 27th position and thereby was the Number One distribution house from India.

## YRF Milestones – 2005

On the auspicious occasion of Dassera (12 October 2005), YRF moved into its newly constructed corporate office and its own state-of-the-art, fully integrated film studio.

YRF was adjudged the Best Production Company and Best Marketing Company in Entertainment at the Apsara Film & Television Producers Guild Awards 2005.

At a glittering ceremony featuring the 'Who's who' of the Indian corporate landscape, Mr. Yash Chopra was awarded the 'Superbrand' Award by Superbrands International.

The Hindi film industry voted Yash Chopra and Aditya Chopra the most powerful people in the Indian Entertainment business. The Yash Raj juggernaut thundered into the number 1 slot in Filmfare's Power List 2005, making it a first – when a production house has been judged more powerful than any superstar.

## YRF Milestones – 2006

FANAA released on 26 May 2006. It was the first time Aamir Khan starred in a YRF film, with Kajol in the female lead, after a gap of a few years.

India's premier entertainment studio, YRF launched 'YRF Merchandise' with the film FANAA.

On 9 November 2006, Microsoft's Worldwide CEO, Steve Ballmer along with Mr. Yash Chopra launched the DRM protected audio streaming enabled YRF website (www.yashrajfilms.com) in Mumbai, marking the advent of a new era for Indian entertainment consumers.

DHOOM:2, second film in the DHOOM series released on 24 November 2006 with the widest release in Indian cinema history with over 1800 prints. It became the highest-grossing Hindi film of 2006 and was also the highest-grossing Hindi film of all time until 2006.

KABUL EXPRESS released on 15 December 2006. YRF launched Kabir Khan as a director in the film KABUL EXPRESS which won the National Award for the Best Debut Film of a Director.

## YRF Milestones – 2007

CHAK DE INDIA released on 10 August 2007 and won hearts worldwide. The inspirational story of the coach taking his girls hockey team to the pinnacle of world hockey touched the chords of viewers all across the world. The film also received excellent mainstream acclaim. Its title song became a sports anthem of the nation.

CHAK DE INDIA was directed by Shimit Amin. It was Shimit's first film with YRF as a director.

The film won the National Award for the Best Film for providing Popular and Wholesome Entertainment

## YRF Milestones – 2008

YRF teamed up with the Walt Disney Studios, world-renowned leaders in entertainment, to create animation feature films. ROADSIDE ROMEO, which was created in association with Walt Disney Studios was released on 24 October 2008.
The film bagged the National Award for the Best Animation Film that year.

Yash Chopra was honoured by the renowned Hollywood Reporter magazine at a function held on 14 October 2008 at Taj Land's End, Mumbai. He was felicitated for his invaluable contribution to Indian Cinema and the impact created by Yash Raj Films in business in the US.

## YRF Milestones – 2009

YRF became the first production house from India to launch its own talent division with a focus to launch some of the freshest and most talented artistes in the industry across all touch points.

In 2001, DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE (DDLJ) broke the continuous-exhibition record, established by the film SHOLAY at Minerva Theatre (5 years: 1975 – 1980). And as in March 2009, DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE had set yet another record by completing 700 WEEKS of continuous play in Mumbai's Maratha Mandir theatre.

## YRF Milestones – 2010

BAND BAAJA BAARAAT released on 10 December 2010. Directed by debutant Maneesh Sharma and starring Anushka Sharma with newcomer Ranveer Singh in the lead roles, the film was a critical and commercial success. It became popular for its fresh subject and screenplay and turned out to be one of the most awarded movies that year.

January, 2010 marked the entry of YRF Television, producing new age Television software for transmission on Sony Entertainment Television - India's leading television broadcaster, ushering a new wave of television viewing experience.

YRF & SOTC signed licensing agreement with Swiss-based brand to invest and offered a customized entertainment experience to Indian film fans visiting Switzerland - Yash Raj Films' Enchanted Journey.

## YRF Milestones – 2011

YRF launched a youth films division, Y-Films in 2011. A studio that promises to deliver kick-ass films of the youth, by the youth, for the youth. And be a platform for talent – both on screen and behind the scenes – who will break and set new rules of storytelling.

Uday Chopra announced the formation of YRF Entertainment, a Hollywood-based production and financing company focused on developing and producing feature films for the U.S. and international marketplace.

Mr. Yash Chopra was honoured with the title of 'Ambassador of Interlaken', in Interlaken, making him the first recipient of this prestigious award. A silver plaque was also dedicated to him which was placed in Interlaken along the Hoheweg - Interlaken's famous main street.

This honour for Yash Chopra had been announced in October 2010 in New Delhi by the Swiss Embassy during which Mr. Chopra was also bestowed the 'Swiss Ambassador's Award'.

- A majestic 'Yash Chopra Suite' was also inaugurated at the Victoria Jungfrau Grand Hotel & Spa, Interlaken's most luxurious hotel.
- A further honour was the 'Yash Chopra Train' which was launched as part of the Jungfrau Railways, Switzerland, possibly the first-of-its-kind tribute.

## YRF Milestones – 2012

ISHAQZAADE released on 11 May 2012. It is a romantic thriller written and directed by Habib Faisal, starring debutant Arjun Kapoor, and Parineeti Chopra who won a National Film Award for the film.

YOMICS, a comic book division of YRF was launched in July 2012, at YRF Studios to give users a unique experience of digital comics from their film catalogue.

EK THA TIGER released on 15 August 2012. It's a romantic thriller starring Salman Khan and Katrina Kaif in lead roles. This was the first time YRF worked with Salman Khan in a film and was the third collaboration of Kabir Khan with YRF after KABUL EXPRESS (2006) and NEW YORK (2009).

JAB TAK HAI JAAN released on 13 November 2012.

Just a few weeks earlier, on his 80th birthday Yash Chopra had announced that it would be his last film as director... and he bid farewell on 21 Oct 2012.

JAB TAK HAI JAAN was Yash Chopra's last labour of love. The film received a number of nominations and awards at award shows. The film was praised for its direction, cinematography and chemistry between the lead actors, with Shah Rukh Khan, Katrina Kaif and Anushka Sharma receiving accolades for their performances. Yash Chopra worked with composer A.R. Rahman and lyricist Gulzar for the first time.

## YRF Milestones – 2013

YRF joined hands with Karol Bagh Saree House (KBSH) to create India's first Bollywood inspired fashion label called Diva'ni – where fashion romances Bollywood. India's leading production house, YRF along with KBSH, aimed to serenade the splendour of Indian cinema through aspirational fashion.

DHOOM:3 released on 20 December 2013, marking itself as the first Indian film to be released in the IMAX motion picture film format with Dolby Atmos surround sound.

The film broke many records on its opening days in India and abroad. Box Office India declared DHOOM:3 'the biggest hit of 2013' after two days of release, the film went on to gross INR 556 crores (US$ 93 million) worldwide, to become the fourth highest-grossing Bollywood film till date.

DHOOM:3 was directed by Vijay Krishna Acharya who had also written for the previous films of the DHOOM franchise.

## YRF Milestones – 2014

YRF forayed into the South with fanfare - the much-awaited entry of YRF into the South was led by the remake of its scintillating and entertaining film, BAND BAAJA BAARAAT [titled as AAHA KALYANAM for southern release], which released in 2010 to critical and popular acclaim.

On 12 December 2014, Aditya Chopra's directorial debut, DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE, completed 1000 weeks at Mumbai's Maratha Mandir cinema, becoming the longest running film in the history of Indian cinema. On this historic day, the lead pair of DDLJ, Shah Rukh Khan and Kajol launched a book based on the film titled 'Aditya Chopra relives DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE as told to Nasreen Munni Kabir', which gave the director's rare insights into the making of the iconic film.

## YRF Milestones – 2015

DUM LAGA KE HAISHA released on 27 February 2015. DUM LAGA KE HAISHA is a romantic comedy directed by Sharat Katariya and produced by Maneesh Sharma. The film stars Ayushmann Khurrana and the debutante Bhumi Pednekar in the lead roles.

The film won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Hindi along with Best Lyrics by Varun Grover for the song Moh Moh Ke Dhaage and Best Female Playback Singer by Monali Thakur for the same song.

## YRF Milestones – 2016

YRF Studios inaugurated its in-house VFX Studio - yFX in February 2016.

yFX has worked on YRF's Salman Khan - Anushka Sharma starrer SULTAN as its first film with many more exciting films to follow. With the addition of yFX, YRF exemplifies a complete film making experience in every possible way where one can walk in with an idea and leave with a complete film or TV show.

In May 2016, the Swiss government honoured the work of Yash Chopra with a special statue, as a mark of gratitude and salutations. A sculpture made of pure bronze, and weighing about 350 kilos, has presented Yash Chopra in an iconic pose of him directing a film.

SULTAN released on 6 July 2016. It is a romantic sports-drama film directed by Ali Abbas Zafar. Produced by Aditya Chopra, the film stars Salman Khan as the title character opposite Anushka Sharma. SULTAN has now become the third highest grossing Hindi cinema of all time and has also broken 35 other records at the worldwide box office.

## YRF Milestones – 2025

Shah Rukh Khan and Kajol unveiled a new bronze statue of Raj & Simran at London's Leicester Square (on 4 December 2025), celebrating their iconic roles in one of Indian cinema’s most beloved films, Aditya Chopra’s DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE (DDLJ).

Come Fall in Love - The DDLJ Musical directed by Aditya Chopra, based on the iconic blockbuster DILWALE DULHANIA LE JAYENGE (that celebrated its 30th anniversary milestone on 20 October 2025), premiered in the UK at the Manchester Opera House on 29 May 2025.

SAIYAARA (released on 18 July 2025) completed 50+ days in theatres, emerging as the highest-grossing love story in Indian cinema, grossing INR 584 Cr, in box office collections worldwide.

The SAIYAARA Music Album crossed 900 million streams across music platforms, within just three months of its release. The Saiyaara title track ranked #4 on Spotify’s Global Top Songs Daily Chart - a first-of-its-kind milestone for an Indian track. It also took the #10 spot on Billboard Global 200.

WAR 2, the sixth film in the YRF Spy Universe (starring Hrithik Roshan, NTR, and Kiara Advani), released worldwide on 14 August 2025 across 95 countries, marking the widest ever release for a YRF Spy Universe film. The film made history as the first Indian title to premiere on Dolby Cinema screens in India, representing a landmark achievement in cinematic storytelling and audience experience in the country.

Popular songs Jag Ghoomeya (SULTAN), Main Yahaan Hoon (VEER-ZAARA), Aankhein Khuli (MOHABBATEIN) and Kajra Re (BUNTY AUR BABLI) crossed 500 million views on YouTube.
