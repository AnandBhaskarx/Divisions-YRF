## Films Directed by Yash Chopra


Over a career spanning more than five decades, Yash Chopra directed 22 feature films that collectively shaped the language of mainstream Hindi cinema. His directorial journey began under the banner of B. R. Films and later evolved into the defining creative identity of Yash Raj Films, establishing benchmarks in romance, drama, music integration, and cinematic scale.


Career Phases (High-Level)

Early Social & Political Cinema (1959–1969)
Films such as *Dhool Ka Phool*, *Dharmputra*, and *Waqt* established Yash Chopra as a director unafraid of socially sensitive themes and ensemble storytelling.

Angry Young Man & Industrial Drama (1973–1978)
With *Deewaar* and *Trishul*, he helped define a generation of cinema shaped by moral conflict, class struggle, and ambition.

 Romantic & Musical Era (1976–1997)
From *Kabhi Kabhie* to *Dil To Pagal Hai*, Yash Chopra became synonymous with romance, poetry, music, and emotional nuance.

Mature Romance & Global Cinema (2004–2012)
*Veer-Zaara* and *Jab Tak Hai Jaan* reflected a refined, globally resonant storytelling voice, cementing his legacy as the master of cinematic romance.
